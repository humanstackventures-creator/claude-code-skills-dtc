# Livera UGC & Community Content Skill

The core doctrine: **blur the line between buyer and promoter.** Most brands operate in two silos — customers buy, influencers promote. Winning brands make customers feel like insiders, and insiders share differently.

> "When customers feel seen, involved, and prioritized, they create better content, build stronger loyalty, and drive more word of mouth than any paid strategy alone."

---

## THE FRAMEWORK — Customers as Influencers

### Why this works for Livera specifically
Livera is a habit product. The daily gummy ritual is inherently shareable — morning routine, wellness stack, "what I take every day" content. The brand needs to own this UGC surface before launch, not scramble for it after. Every early adopter who posts is worth more than a mid-tier paid creator.

---

## 8 TACTICAL LEVERS (with Livera applications)

### 1. Community-Driven Product Reveals
**Blueprint brand:** Poppi — let customers drive flavor reveal, not influencers. Created participatory hype vs. manufactured reach.

**Livera application:**
- Pre-launch: tease the product to email subscribers and engaged IG followers *before* any influencer seeding
- Frame them as "first to know" — not as a marketing blast but as insider access
- Ask them to share a specific reaction: "Tag us if you're in"
- This creates organic hype that feels earned, not paid

### 2. Early Testing & Feedback Loops
**Blueprint brand:** Experiment — Lab Rat / Lab X program where real customers test formulations before launch. Customers as co-creators reduce launch risk and increase advocacy.

**Livera application:**
- Send the 90-day bundle to 20–30 engaged followers (not influencers — people who've commented, saved, DM'd) 3–4 weeks before launch
- Frame: "You're our Lab Rat. Tell us everything."
- Capture: photos, videos, honest reactions
- Use feedback publicly (with permission): "Our testers said X. We listened."
- Their content = authentic UGC for paid ads

### 3. PR List Prioritization by Affinity, Not Followers
**Blueprint brand:** Liaison The Label — loyal community members on PR lists *before* influencers. Qualification: genuine affinity, not follower count.

**Livera application:**
- Build a "Livera Insider" list of the first 100 people who engage with the account (comments, saves, DM responses)
- These people get product before any paid creator
- The signal this sends: loyalty is rewarded here
- These early recipients generate content with zero follower gate — authenticity > reach at this stage

### 4. Inclusive Brand Experiences (No Follower Gate)
**Blueprint brand:** Waterboy — opened a Tulum trip to all via TikTok hashtag, judged on creativity not followers. Volume of UGC became cultural moment.

**Livera application (scaled down for pre-launch):**
- Run a "Why does your liver need Livera?" UGC challenge pre-launch
- Entry requirement: post a Story or Reel tagging @liverawellness — no follower requirement
- Prize: free 90-day bundle (1–3 winners)
- Result: content, email leads, community signal

### 5. Early Access Shopping for Loyalty
**Blueprint brand:** Set Active — most loyal customers get first access to drops via broadcast channel and rewards program.

**Livera application:**
- Launch day: Livera Insider list gets the link 24 hours before public launch
- Broadcast channel (IG) or email-only access window
- Messaging: "You've been following along — you go first."
- Psychology: exclusivity earned through loyalty, not follower count

### 6. Community Spotlights on Brand Social
**Blueprint brand:** Parke — consistent spotlighting of real customers on brand feed, regardless of follower count. Makes brand feed feel like a community, not a catalog.

**Livera application:**
- After launch: one "community spotlight" post per week on IG
- Feature: a real customer, their morning routine, why they take Livera
- Zero follower requirement — pick based on authentic story
- Format: carousel with their quote + their photo + their result
- Effect: future customers see themselves represented; current customers aspire to be featured

### 7. Members-Only Real-Life Events
**Blueprint brand:** Glowbar — pilates classes and wellness walks for real community, no paid influencers allowed.

**Livera application (Phase 2+, Miami):**
- "Liver health" wellness morning event — run in Miami post-launch
- Open only to subscribers, not press/influencers
- Generates real-world content and emotional tie to brand
- File this for Month 3+

### 8. Personalized High-Touch Gestures
**Blueprint brand:** Refy — hand-delivered products in the UK. Not scalable; impact is emotional.

**Livera application:**
- For the first 10 customers who subscribe: personal handwritten note + extra gummies
- Lucas or team personally DMs to say thank you
- Cost: $20. Return: content, loyalty signal, word of mouth
- "I got a personal note from the founder" = content

## UGC CREATOR BRIEF TEMPLATE

Use this when briefing creators (paid or gifted). Brief the *behavior*, not the script.

```
BRIEF: Livera UGC

PRODUCT: Livera Liver Detox Gummies — 2 gummies/day, zero sugar, 100% organic
GOAL: Authentic daily habit content — not an ad, an observation

WHAT TO SHOW:
- Your morning routine, with the gummies as one natural part of it
- The specific reason you need liver support (bloating, energy crash, brain fog, hormones — pick your truth)
- After 2–3 weeks: a specific thing you noticed (energy, skin, bloating)

FORMAT: iPhone-quality, hand-held, no ring light, no script. Speak like you're texting a friend.

WHAT NOT TO DO:
- Don't say "I was gifted" in the video (disclosure in caption is fine)
- Don't read from notes
- Don't use the word "detox" as a selling point
- Don't hold the product to camera like a product demo

HOOK OPTIONS (pick one):
- "The one supplement I actually take every day—"
- "I started taking these 3 weeks ago because [specific problem]"
- "My liver needed this more than I realized"

CAPTION NOTE: Short and real. "Tag someone whose liver needs a break" works. "This supplement transformed my life" does not.

DELIVERABLES: 1 Reel (15–30s) + 3 Stories. Usage rights: paid ads and organic repost.
```

## WHAT MAKES LIVERA UGC WORK vs. FAIL

| Works | Fails |
|---|---|
| Specific symptom ("my 2pm crash stopped") | Generic wellness ("feel amazing!") |
| Real morning routine context | Holding product to camera in blank room |
| Honest "I was skeptical" opener | Overclaiming detox/transformation |
| Creator's actual reason for needing it | Script-reading |
| iPhone, natural light | Over-produced "unboxing" energy |
| Short (15–30s) | Long explanation of ingredients |

## METRICS TO TRACK FOR UGC

- **Save rate** (primary) — indicates "I want to come back to this"
- **Share rate** — indicates "I want someone else to see this"
- **Comment quality** — "What is this?" = discovery; "I have the same problem" = resonance
- **Paid ad CTR when used as creative** — UGC vs. polished ad (Grüns proved UGC wins)
