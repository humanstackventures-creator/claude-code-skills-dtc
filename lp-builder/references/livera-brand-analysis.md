# Livera — Brand Reference Analysis

Compiled from Tier 1, 2, and 3 brand references. Use this to derive Livera's
merged brand voice, visual direction, and LP structure.

## Tier 1 Brands (Primary Inspiration)

### AG1 (drinkag1.com)
- **Voice:** Authoritative yet accessible. Science-backed without jargon.
- **Hero:** Celebrity-anchored headline + dual CTAs ("Get Started" / "Learn More") + video bg
- **Problem framing:** Nutrition as gap-filling — "87% of individuals had at least 1 dietary nutrient gap." Never says "you're unhealthy," frames as optimization.
- **Solution:** "75+ quality ingredients" — positioned as replacement, not addition. Four clinical trials front-and-center.
- **Pricing:** Subscription-first ($79/mo vs $99 one-time). Cost-per-serving framing ("less than $3/day"). 90-day money-back guarantee.
- **Colors:** Teal-green (#0c3d3d), white/off-white, earth tones.
- **Copy patterns:** Ownership language in testimonials, paradox hooks ("More than a greens powder"), benefit + ease pairings, dense social proof ("50,000+ verified 5-star reviews").
- **Key takeaway:** Science-first credibility + lifestyle integration. Subscription normalized as daily ritual.

### Graza (graza.co)
- **Voice:** Conversational, playful, ingredient-focused. Premium but unpretentious.
- **Hero:** Product-focused headline ("Mayo made with only the good stuff") + "SHOP NOW" CTA.
- **Problem framing:** Implied dissatisfaction — "the way it should be" suggests existing products fall short.
- **Solution:** Branded product names (Drizzle, Sizzle, Frizzle) with clear use-case guidance.
- **Pricing:** Tiered ($14-$65), bundles encouraged, refill system.
- **Colors:** Vibrant lime green (#D1E030), warm cream (#F6E6D9), olive tones.
- **Copy patterns:** Short declarative statements, casual confidence, naming as hooks.
- **Key takeaway:** Restraint in claims. Let product quality speak. Warm, lived-in luxury.

### Everyday Dose (everydaydose.com)
- **Voice:** (Page loaded as A/B testing JS — limited data)
- **Key takeaway:** Sophisticated A/B testing infrastructure. Price variation testing, visitor segmentation. Shows importance of conversion optimization.

### Jolie (jolieskinco.com)
- **Voice:** Sophisticated yet approachable. Calm, health-focused, aspirational.
- **Hero:** Promotional banner first, minimal hero — prioritizes immediate CTAs.
- **Problem framing:** Implied, not stated. "The most fundamental part of your daily routine."
- **Solution:** Core product ($169) + subscription bundle ($152 + $35/quarter filters).
- **Colors:** Black, white, warm metallics (gold, chrome). Minimalist luxury.
- **Copy patterns:** Benefit-forward ("better skin, hair, and wellbeing"), short sentences, functional specificity.
- **Key takeaway:** Restraint in design. No testimonial bombardment. Premium = whitespace + typography.

## Tier 2 Brands (Secondary Inspiration)

### Ritual (ritual.com)
- **Voice:** Sophisticated yet accessible. Educated, health-conscious, not preachy.
- **Hero:** Promotional banner lead ("Save 20%"), category-based browsing.
- **Problem framing:** Implied through collection names (Gut Health, Skin, Performance). Assumes health literacy.
- **Solution:** "Targeted interventions" not generic vitamins. Clinical naming (Synbiotic+, Hyacera).
- **Pricing:** Subscription discount model (20% single, 30% bundles). Pricing hidden until cart.
- **Colors:** Navy (#142B6F), gold (#FFD600), cream (#FEF6EB). Premium minimalism.
- **Copy patterns:** Benefit-driven headlines, specificity markers, credibility signaling.
- **Key takeaway:** Informed consumer brand. Ingredient transparency + lifecycle segmentation.

### ARMRA (armra.com)
- **Voice:** Aspirational, regenerative. "The Revival of Health."
- **Problem framing:** Modern health depletion — foundational wellness through ancestral approaches.
- **Solution:** Colostrum-based, natural origins, traditional health adapted for modern use.
- **Pricing:** Promotional bundles (30% OFF + 2 FREE GIFTS + FREE SHIPPING welcome kits).
- **Colors:** Neutral, refined. Dark grays/blacks. Minimalist.
- **Key takeaway:** Storytelling over hard sell. Narrative around wellness restoration.

### LMNT (drinklmnt.com)
- **Voice:** Rebellious yet authoritative. "More Salt, Not Less." Counter-conventional.
- **Hero:** Product-first (four sparkling cans) + "INTRODUCING 12oz SPARKLING" + "Get Yours" CTA.
- **Problem framing:** Reframes conventional wisdom as flawed. Cites JAMA research.
- **Solution:** Three-tier (packets + cans), 14 flavors, no-sugar/no-junk positioning.
- **Pricing:** $45/box single, $39/box subscription (13.33% off).
- **Colors:** Vibrant fruit-inspired — citrus yellow, grapefruit pink, watermelon magenta.
- **Copy patterns:** Founder storytelling, pattern repetition ("Stay Salty"), specificity over generics, active voice.
- **Key takeaway:** Founder authenticity + scientific evidence + irreverent brand voice justifies premium.

## Cross-Brand Patterns (Use These for Livera)

### Voice & Tone Patterns
1. **Science-backed but accessible** — cite research, avoid jargon (AG1, Ritual, LMNT)
2. **Confident without being preachy** — state quality, don't over-explain (Graza, Jolie)
3. **Conversational with personality** — short sentences, active voice, occasional playfulness (Graza, LMNT)
4. **Aspirational framing** — wellness as optimization, not fixing broken people (AG1, ARMRA)

### Hero Patterns
1. Big product shot is non-negotiable across all brands
2. Headline is either short/punchy OR benefit-driven — never generic
3. Single primary CTA + optional secondary
4. Promotional banner above fold is common (Ritual, Jolie, ARMRA)

### Problem Framing Patterns
1. **Never fear-based** — frame as gaps, not failures
2. **Implied dissatisfaction** works (Graza, Jolie) — "the way it should be"
3. **Data-backed urgency** works (AG1, LMNT) — cite stats, not emotions
4. Icons/visuals help break up problem points

### Pricing Patterns
1. Subscription always highlighted as default/recommended
2. Cost-per-serving or cost-per-day framing (AG1)
3. Welcome kits / bundle discounts as conversion levers (AG1, ARMRA)
4. Money-back guarantee is table stakes

### Visual Patterns
1. Clean, generous whitespace
2. Product photography is hero-quality — never stock-feeling
3. Limited color palette (2-3 colors max)
4. Sans-serif typography dominates
5. Icons for features/benefits, not decorative illustration
6. Sticky nav is universal
